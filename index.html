The Dancing Plague of 1518: A Historical Enigma

The Bizarre Outbreak

In July 1518, the city of Strasbourg (then part of the Holy Roman Empire, now in France) witnessed a peculiar and alarming phenomenon. A woman named Frau Troffea began dancing fervently in the streets. Within days, dozens, and eventually hundreds, joined her, dancing uncontrollably for days or even weeks. This event, known as the Dancing Plague of 1518, persisted for about two months before subsiding .
